/* Notes:
- Structure reflects the paper?s reduced IL-6 signaling (binding to IL-6R or sIL-6R; recruitment of gp130; STAT3 phosphorylation) embedded in liver and GI compartments, plus circulation (Serum).
- sgp130 sequesters IL-6:sIL-6R in Serum.
- CRP is produced (in Serum compartment) as a Michaelis?Menten function of liver pSTAT3; sIL-6R production is accelerated by CRP (shedding), consistent with text.
- All major soluble species have basal production and first-order degradation; membrane receptors have turnover.
- Exchange between Serum and organs is modeled by bidirectional linear transport.
- Enzymatic steps (STAT3 phosphorylation/dephosphorylation and pSTAT3-driven expression) use Michaelis?Menten; all other processes are mass action, as specified.
- Initial biomarker levels in nmol/L were converted from Table 1 (Crohn?s baseline) using the reported MWs.
- Parameter values provided here are placeholders to ensure an executable model; the paper reports fitting/optimization details in supplementary material not included in the input. */
